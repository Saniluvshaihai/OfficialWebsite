Year 1 - 3

Queen Margaret College aims to install a love and understanding of digital technology right from the lower levels of the school. These students' programmes are comprised of a combination of digital and hardcopy learning. This allows them to gain the knowledge of how to use these devices, a crucial skill required for today's digital world. At this level, students are using the school iPads for coding, and there is an extension group for this after school. Students learn to use technology to improve logical thinking, problem solving and creativity. Classes are installed with both next-generation interactive whiteboards and iPads. The teachers and students are very knowledgeable about this technology and use it frequently.

Year 4

Year 4 students take steps to further their technological knowledge and ability. Students have a combination of iPads and Chromebooks available to them which they use both individually and in small groups. This is a transitional year for this age level and students move from typing on a screen to typing on a keyboard. These devices are used for day-to-day work as the curriculum becomes more challenging. At this level, students also learn about coding and begin to use to Lego Spike to improve computational thinking.

Year 5 - 6

In year 5, the BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) programme commences. Students own their laptops and are reliant on them for research, presentations, and assessment. The students look at digital ethics and how to stay safe online as well as how to use their laptops to their full potential and capacity. Year 6 Students participate in the PYP exhibition and during this inquiry students are learning to develop, manipulate, store, retrieve, and share digital content. Students think critically about a concept and record parts of the process on their devices. Deeper computational thinking and research skills are taught to the students.
